# Target Users

## User Segmentation

| User Type | Description | Needs | Problems | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Amateur endurance athletes | Users who regularly practice sports such as running, cycling, trail running or triathlon | Track workouts, view history, analyze progress, monitor performance | Existing apps may be too complex or rely heavily on online services | High |
| Semi-professional athletes | More experienced athletes who want additional performance information | Training statistics, activity analysis, performance tracking | May require advanced tools that are expensive or specialized | High |
| Beginners | Users starting a new sport activity | Simple workout registration, motivation, progress visibility | Lack of knowledge about performance tracking | Medium |
| Fitness-oriented users | Users focused on maintaining an active lifestyle | Weekly activity tracking and personal goals | Do not need competitive or social features | Medium |
| Professional athletes | High-performance athletes with advanced requirements | Detailed analytics, coaching tools, professional metrics | This MVP does not provide enough specialized functionality | Low |

---

## Primary Target User

The primary target user is:

**Amateur and semi-professional endurance athletes**

This group is prioritized because they usually need:

- Training history
- Performance statistics
- Progress tracking
- Integration with platforms such as Strava

---

## Out of Scope Users

| User Type | Reason |
|---|---|
| Professional athletes | Require advanced analysis and professional coaching tools |
| Coaches | Need athlete management features |
| Users looking for social networks | Social features are not part of the MVP |

---

## Conclusion

The application focuses on providing a simple and low-cost sports tracking solution for individual athletes.

The MVP prioritizes personal training management over social features or professional-level analytics.
